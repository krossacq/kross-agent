# SOP 29: Client-Facing Next Steps From Calls

Version: 0.1
Owner: Tax Mogul Bureau
Applies to: Kross Agent for Firms
Phase: 4 - Meeting Intelligence

## Purpose

Define how Kross Agent creates client-facing next steps from a call summary.

## Core Rule

Client-facing next steps must be approved before they become visible to the client or are sent through GHL.

## Client-Facing Next Steps May Include

Kross may draft next steps such as:

- upload requested document
- answer a specific open question
- review a quote
- pay invoice
- schedule follow-up call
- confirm identity/contact information
- complete organizer section
- wait for preparer review

## Client-Facing Next Steps Must Not Include

Kross must not include:

- internal preparer notes
- AI draft notes
- raw AI reasoning
- firm SOPs
- private pricing logic unless approved
- another client's information
- unapproved tax conclusions
- sensitive risk flags that are not meant for the client view
- company-private Tax Mogul Bureau build details

## Drafting Rules

Client-facing next steps should be:

- specific
- plain-language
- respectful
- limited to the client's action
- free of unnecessary internal detail
- connected to a source
- reviewed before sending or publishing

## Workflow

1. Generate call summary.
2. Extract possible client-facing next steps.
3. Remove internal-only notes.
4. Check for sensitive or uncertain items.
5. Draft client-facing version.
6. Send to preparer/admin for review.
7. Human approves, edits, or rejects.
8. Approved next steps appear in the client portal or become a draft GHL message.
9. Log approval and publication/sending.

## Approval Required

Human approval is required when next steps:

- mention refund/balance-due
- mention tax eligibility or compliance risk
- request sensitive documents
- include deadlines
- discuss pricing, quote, or invoice
- will be sent through GHL
- will become visible in the client portal

## What Kross Must Not Do

Kross must not:

- publish next steps automatically without approval
- turn internal notes into client-facing text
- pressure clients with unsupported claims
- promise refund amounts
- claim a return is complete unless preparer approved
- reveal system or company-private information

## Audit Requirements

Log:

- next steps drafted
- next steps edited
- next steps approved
- next steps rejected
- next steps made client-visible
- next steps sent through GHL

## Acceptance Criteria

This SOP is working when:

- client-facing next steps are separated from internal notes
- approval happens before visibility or sending
- next steps are tied to the correct client
- sensitive or uncertain items are escalated
